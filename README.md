## What Counts as a "Top Proxy" Anyway?

A top proxy is a paid or free IP-routing service that meets four practical tests: high uptime (typically 99%+), believable IP reputation (residential or ISP-grade for tougher targets), enough geographic coverage for the use case, and pricing that doesn't bankrupt small projects. Anything missing one of those legs will frustrate you eventually.

Different jobs need different kinds of proxies. Datacenter IPs are fast and cheap but easier to detect. Residential IPs come from real consumer devices and slip past most defenses. ISP proxies are a hybrid — datacenter sped wrapped in residential reputation. Mobile proxies route through cellular cariers and are the hardest to block, but they're also the priciest by a wide margin.

Quick recap: pick datacenter for general scraping of unprotected sites, residential for sneakers and ad verification, ISP for account management and SEO tools, and mobile only when nothing else gets through.
